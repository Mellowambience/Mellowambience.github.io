About

Natalie Cohen, OTR/L

I'm an occupational therapist with a passion for the intersection of health, identity, and the small-but-mighty moments that define how we move through the world. OT, to me, is never just about function — it's about meaning.

I work at the edge of the clinical and the human: helping clients build or rebuild the daily occupations that anchor their sense of self. Whether that's returning to work after injury, adapting a morning routine, or simply rediscovering joy in a task once taken for granted.

Outside the clinic I write — about OT, about bodies, about what it means to live well inside the life you actually have. Eventually this space will also be home to my own practice. For now, it's a place to think out loud.

OT Practice

What occupational therapy actually is

OT is one of the most misunderstood professions in healthcare — and one of the most quietly powerful. Here's what I believe it's really about.

🌊

Occupation as Identity

We are what we do. The things we engage in — how we care for ourselves, connect with others, contribute to the world — are how we know ourselves.

🐚

Holistic by Design

OT sees the whole person — mind, body, environment, context. Every treatment plan is tailored to what a specific person, in a specific life, actually needs.

🪸

Meaning Over Mechanics

It's not just about regaining movement or building a skill. It's about why that movement matters — and who you get to be again once you have it.

🌿
